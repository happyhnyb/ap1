The palm oil market is currently a masterclass in market psychology versus structural reality. As we move through the final week of February 2026, the Crude Palm Oil (CPO) complex is under sustained pressure, grappling with a fundamental shift in export velocity and a cooling of the "policy fever" that defined the market's bullish run late last year. The Price Reality: Testing the RM 4,000 Support The bearish momentum on the Bursa Malaysia Derivatives (BMD) is no longer speculative. Benchmark futures are aggressively testing the RM 4,000 per tonne floor—a level that served as a launchpad in 2025 but now acts as a ceiling. This retreat from the RM 4,150 highs of early February is driven by a stark reality: supply is catching up to demand, and the "premium" once attributed to scarcity is evaporating. The "Biofuel De-escalation" Factor For months, the market was buoyed by Indonesia’s ambitious B50 biodiesel mandate. However, recent signals from Jakarta suggesting a tactical delay in full implementation until the latter half of 2026 have triggered a significant "policy de-risking" event. The Scarcity Premium Fades: Without the immediate draw of millions of additional tonnes into the domestic Indonesian energy pool, the exportable surplus is higher than analysts projected for Q1 2026. Energy Spillovers: The POGO (Palm Oil - Gasoil) spread has narrowed, making palm-based biodiesel less economically attractive for discretionary blending, further weighing on the industrial price floor.

The Competitiveness Gap: The Bean Oil Variable While palm remains the world's most consumed vegetable oil, its dominance is being challenged by the Soybean Oil (SBO) complex. Narrowing Spreads: The BOPO (Bean Oil - Palm Oil) spread is currently hovering near $100-110/tonne. For price-sensitive buyers, this narrow spread is a "danger zone" for palm. The China Export Pivot: China’s transition into a net exporter of soybean oil has flooded the Asian market with cheap soy alternatives. This "Soy-Saturations" is directly competing with palm in traditional strongholds, preventing any meaningful CPO price recovery. Logistics and Export Velocity The data for February confirms the slowdown. Cargo surveyors indicate that Malaysian palm oil exports for the first 20 days of February fell by 9%+ mom. Post-Lunar New Year and Ramadan demand over, restocking has been tepid, and high port stocks in India are limiting new bulk purchases. Strategic Outlook: The Search for a Bottom As we look toward the second quarter of 2026, we are approaching the seasonal high-production window. If yields hold, a supply wall will hit the market by May. The takeaway? Palm oil is no longer in a "supply-side squeeze" but a "demand-side struggle." Success for traders in 2026 will mean looking past the policy headlines and focusing on the cold, hard data of export shipments and the relative value of the bean oil spread.
