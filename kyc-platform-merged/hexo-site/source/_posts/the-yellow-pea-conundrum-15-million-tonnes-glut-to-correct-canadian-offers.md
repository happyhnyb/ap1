The global pulse market is currently a masterclass in the "supply-heavy" cycle. As we move into April 2026, the narrative for Canadian yellow pea exporters has shifted from "How much can we ship?" to "How thin can the margin go?" With an anticipated 5-8% correction in Indian CFR prices, the math between the Canadian prairie and the Indian port is getting uncomfortably tight. The Global Glut: A 15-Million-Tonne Shadow We are currently navigating the aftermath of a massive 2025-26 crop year. Global production surged to 15.1 million tonnes (Mt), up roughly 11% year-over-year. Canada: The "Heavyweight" produced a record 3.93 Mt (up 31%) dry peas. This bounty came with a price: record carry-out stocks of 1.31 Mt. Even with a projected drop to 2.95 Mt for 2026-27 due to reduced seeding, the current overhang is massive. Russia: The "Disruptor" hit ~4.7 Mt. Russia remains the low-cost benchmark, aggressively pricing into China and forcing Canadian exporters to match or lose share. USA & EU: Combined with the US and strong EU outputs, there is simply a "glut". The Destination Math: Why India will be Correcting India is seeing intense arrival pressure from a near-record domestic winter pulse crop. While yellow peas are a supplement, they must compete with local prices and larger than required carry. Currently, delivered prices in India sit around ₹41/kg, but a correction to ₹38/kg looks a strong

possibility. When you factor in the 30% import duty and recent Rupee volatility, the "India Math" forces a revision of the CFR target to $310–$315 USD/t. The Origin Math: The Margin Squeeze For a Canadian exporter, the "Reverse Math" from that $310 CFR target is brutal: Spot (Old Crop): Trading at CAD 8.00–8.50/bu. At-Track/Origin: ~$210–$223 USD/t. FOB Vancouver: ~$285–$298 USD/t. New Crop (Forward): Trading at CAD 7.00–8.00/bu. At-Track/Origin: ~$185–$210 USD/t. FOB Vancouver: ~$260–$285 USD/t. The Gap: If the Indian CFR must sit at $310–$315, and ocean freight remains sticky, a $298 FOB price leaves virtually zero room for error, insurance, or profit. The Outlook: A 5-8% "Reality Hit" The anticipated 5-8% correction in Indian CFR is essentially the market's way of telling Canadian exporters that the "scarcity premium" is officially dead. While China’s recent removal of the 100% anti-dumping tariff provides a much-needed relief valve for volume, it isn't necessarily a savior for price. The Bottom Line: We are in a "Volume over Value" phase. Exporters will need to be hyper-efficient with logistics to survive the sub-$315 CFR environment.
