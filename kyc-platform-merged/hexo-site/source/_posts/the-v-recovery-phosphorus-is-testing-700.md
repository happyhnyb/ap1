The phosphate market is currently navigating a high-volatility "V-recovery." For those managing ag-input portfolios, the recent 10.4% surge in DAP prices—climbing from $625/t (Feb 27) to $690/t (Mar 23)—is more than a seasonal blip; it’s a structural warning. 1. The Price Architecture: 2022 vs. 2025 vs. 2026 Understanding the current $690/t level requires looking at the "Ghost of Peaks Past": March 2022 ($1,005/t): An energy-driven crisis. Natural gas spiked, a component of DAP the primary cost driver. August 2025 ($800/t): A geopolitical supply-chain shock. Initial Middle East tensions led to the first major "Sulphur Crunch." March 2026 ($690/t): A freight and logistics "war premium." Shipping reliability has collapsed, natural gas is spiking, sulphur is stuck and less said about energy the better. 2. Supply & Demand: The Fragile Balance Production Capacity: Global MAP/DAP output is ~67M tonnes. While rock is abundant, finished fertilizer is bottlenecked by raw material costs. The Consumption Core: Cereals and grains account for 46.2% of global P-usage. The New Competitor: LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) batteries. Industrial demand for purified phosphoric acid is growing at 10% CAGR, effectively cannibalizing supply that previously went to the fertilizer sector. 3. Global Trade: The Power Players The market is increasingly concentrated, making it sensitive to localized shocks: The Exporters: Morocco (OCP) controls 70% of global reserves. China remains the "spoiler," with

export quotas frequently removing 3-5M tonnes from the spot market to protect domestic prices. The Importers: India (6-7M tonnes/year) and Brazil (4.5M tonnes/year) remain the price setters. 4. Key Macro Drivers for Q2 2026 The Sulphur Chokehold: Middle Eastern producers supply 45%+ of traded sulphur. With the Strait of Hormuz under pressure, even non-conflict producers like Morocco are struggling to source the sulphuric acid needed to process rock into DAP. "Mining the Soil": At $700/t, we are seeing "demand destruction", unlike seen in previous spikes. Farmers in Latin America and Sub-Saharan Africa are "skipping" P-applications. Short-term yields may hold, but the long-term risk to soil health and 2027 yields is significant. Strategic Outlook The direction is clear: Bullish on price, bearish on liquidity. Unless a maritime "Safe Passage" for fertilizers and key raw materials is established, DAP/MAP is likely to test peaks by mid-quarter.
