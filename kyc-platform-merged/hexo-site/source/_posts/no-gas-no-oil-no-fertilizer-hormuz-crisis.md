The WTO/AXSMarine Strait of Hormuz Trade Tracker confirms a systemic collapse in maritime traffic following the February 28, 2026, closure. The data reflects a transition from geopolitical tension to a total physical blockade of one of the world’s most vital trade arteries. Key Data Insights: The Energy & Fertilizer "Break": Outbound shipments of Crude Oil, LNG, and fertilizer precursors (ammonia and sulphur) plummeted to near zero by March 2. This immediate halt has removed significant daily volumes from the global market, directly fueling the 30%+ surge in urea prices and the sharp rise in energy futures. The cessation of fertilizer inputs is particularly critical for Northern Hemisphere planting windows. The Inbound Agricultural Lag: While outbound traffic stopped instantly, the Inbound Agricultural Index (measured by discharge date) showed a temporary lag. This reflects the unloading of vessels that entered the Strait before the February 28 deadline. As this "pre-arrival" inventory finishes discharging, the region faces a severe supply-side cliff for food security starting in April. AIS Integrity: While the tracker monitors AIS-traceable vessels, the scale of the drop suggests that commercial shipping at scale has effectively abandoned the route. Any "dark" transits represent a negligible fraction of the former volume. Conclusion: The Strait has ceased to function as a global trade corridor. The total halt in fertilizer precursors and hydrocarbons creates a global multiplier effect, moving the crisis from a logistics delay to a fundamental production and inflation emergency.

Trade Momentum Index (March 24, 2026): Outbound Crude/LNG: Near Zero (Total Halt) Fertilizer Precursors: Near Zero (Supply Chain Break) Inbound Agriculture: Declining (Lagged Disruption)
