For too long, food and agriculture were treated as “soft” issues — important for rural livelihoods and development, but peripheral to the real engines of global power. That era is over. In 2026, food has moved to the center of macroeconomics, geopolitics, and capital allocation. It is no longer just about farm output. It is about inflation, political stability, sovereign risk, trade leverage, and strategic resilience. Capital is beginning to understand before politics that food is not a side story to the global economy but foundations of stability in an unstable century.
