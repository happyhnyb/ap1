The Indian Kabuli Chana market is facing a structural "Logistics Siege." Despite a healthy harvest, the physical movement of this high-value crop is being throttled by the ongoing conflict in the Persian Gulf. The Headline Data: The 15% Export Dip For the 2025-26 season, India’s Kabuli Chana exports are forecasted to dip by 15%, falling to 0.11 MMT from the previous year's 0.13 MMT. This is not a production failure, but a logistical paralysis. 1. The Production Ledger: Niche but Vital Total Chickpea Output: 11.7 MMT (Rabi 2025-26). Kabuli Chana Share: ~0.5 MMT (only 4.3% of volume, but a major export revenue driver). Hubs: Maharashtra (Latur, Akola) and Madhya Pradesh (Indore, Dewas). The Quality Gap: While the 0.5 MMT volume is intact, "Bold" counts (12mm+) are lower due to February heatwaves, commanding 15-20% size-premium. 2. The Logistics Pincer: A 300% "War Tax" The primary obstacle is the physical blockade of the Strait of Hormuz and Red Sea risks. Freight Explosion: Ocean freight rates to Middle East/Mediterranean hubs have surged by 300%. Voyage Delays: Rerouting via the Cape of Good Hope has added 20–30 days to transit times, triggering massive war-risk insurance premiums. Container Scarcity: A shortage of 20ft boxes at Inland Container Depots (ICDs) like Nagpur and Indore has added a Rs. 2,000/tonne "positioning premium."

3. Market Dynamics & Competition The Middle East Anchor: The UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Iran traditionally absorb 45% of exports. With Ramadan 2026 ending today, an estimated 20,000 tonnes missed the peak demand window and remain "stranded." The Mexican/Russian Squeeze: Mexico is outcompeting Indian 12mm+ in high-end markets, while Russia is dumping smaller calibres at a 10% discount. Currency Neutralization: The 94 INR/USD exchange rate provides an 4-8% buffer, but this has been entirely swallowed by the freight hike. 4. The Mandi Reality: Domestic Price Divergence Logistics issues are creating a "Price Divorce" between origin and destination. Mandi Prices: Trading between Rs. 7,600 and Rs. 8,000 per quintal in Indore and Jalgaon. Exporters' Retreat: Unable to pass on freight costs, exporters have lowered bid prices, forcing farmers to hold back stocks (arrivals are down 25% YoY). Strategic Takeaway India’s 0.5 MMT Kabuli crop is currently a "stranded asset." Without a reopen of shipping lanes, the domestic market faces a potential glut of export-grade bold seeds, which could crash local mandi prices by 12–15% by Q3.
