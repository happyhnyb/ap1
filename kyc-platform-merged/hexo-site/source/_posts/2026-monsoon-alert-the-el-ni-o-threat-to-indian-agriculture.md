The global climate cycle is shifting, and the implications for Indian agriculture are severe. The latest April 2026 NOAA confirms that the Pacific is transitioning from a weak La Niña to ENSO- neutral, with a high probability of a powerful El Niño developing during the monsoon. The projected outcome? A subpar Indian Southwest Monsoon at 94% of the Long Period Average (LPA). While a delicately positive Indian Ocean Dipole (IOD) might offer slight initial buffering, El Niño will dominate the second half of the season. Agriculture must prepare for prolonged breaks, intense heatwaves, and heightened drought risks across central, western, and northwestern India—paradoxically coupled with extreme daily rainfall bursts in core zones like the Western Ghats and central-eastern India. NOAA’s Escalating El Niño Probabilities (April 2026 Data):📊 The transition is accelerating rapidly, with high persistence expected through the end of the year: • Apr–Jun: 20% • May–Jul: 61% • Jun–Aug: 79% • Jul–Sep: 87% • Aug–Oct: 90% • Sep–Nov: 92% • Oct–Dec: 93%

Month-Wise Outlook:🗓️ JUNE (Onset Phase - 101% LPA)🌱 Probability: 40-50% chance of normal. Low El Niño interference. Normal onset expected. Favorable early rains in southern, coastal, and central India will support early Kharif sowing. Northwest and western India should see normal initial showers one must heavily capitalize on this early, unhindered planting window. JULY (Build-Up Phase - 95% LPA)☀️ Probability: 40-50% chance of below-normal rains. El Niño's grip tightens. Weakening monsoon flow will trigger breaks and potentially delay northward progression. Central and western India will face soil moisture stress, threatening crop establishment. Monitor heat stress closely. AUGUST (Peak Monsoon - 92% LPA)📉 Probability: ~60% chance of below-normal conditions. Peak structural suppression. With El Niño probability soaring, expect widespread dry spells in rainfed regions (Rajasthan, MP, Maharashtra, Gujarat). This poses a massive threat to Kharif crops (paddy, pulses, soyabean, cotton) during their critical growth stage. SEPTEMBER (Withdrawal Phase - 89% LPA)🍂 Probability: ~70% chance of below-normal. Early monsoon withdrawal is highly likely. Sharp shortfalls in the northwest and central belts pose severe damage risks during the crop maturation phase. Expect yields to contract. OCTOBER (Retreat Phase)🏜️ Probability: 70-80% chance of below-normal post-monsoon rains. Lingering drought effects. Reduced cyclonic activity means the northeast monsoon may broadly underperform, offering limited relief for southern India and heavily compromising baseline soil moisture for Rabi sowing. Note: Official IMD spatial and quantitative forecasts will further refine this trajectory in mid- April and late May.
