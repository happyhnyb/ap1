The global agricultural supply chain is currently facing a highly specialized, unprecedented crisis. It is not just about natural gas, urea, amonia or potash; right now, the world is rapidly running out of accessible sulfur. This severe shortage of sulfuric acid—a critical chemical precursor required to process raw phosphate rock—is forcing major global fertilizer producers to scale back operations, idle plants, and absorb massive margin compression. The sulfur deficit is paralyzing the industry's largest players. The Global Giants Under Pressure:🌎 The Mosaic Company (Brazil/USA): Mosaic is severely exposed. They have been forced to idle 🇧🇷 single super phosphate (SSP) production at their Fospar and Araxá facilities in Brazil. To avoid ruinous, record-high spot prices, they have completely suspended open-market sulfur purchases, relying solely on dwindling long-term contracts. OCP Group (Morocco): The absolute titan of global phosphates is feeling the pinch. OCP is 🇲🇦 advancing scheduled maintenance across several plants, effectively stripping up to 30% of its production capacity in Q2 2026 to manage the exceptionally tight sulfur and ammonia supply. PhosAgro (Russia): Facing severe internal sulfur shortages as Gazprom's technical sulfur 🇷🇺 production (at Astrakhan and Orenburg) plummets. PhosAgro has been pushed to request a temporary price cap as margins for non-integrated DAP fertilizers have plunged into negative territory. Sinochem (China): Beijing is playing defense. Sinochem will halt sulfuric acid exports as 🇨🇳 government has banned exports starting May. Production of DAP/MAP is dipping and no exports

quota are provided to rigorously preserve domestic stocks for their own national food security. Coromandel International & Indian Importers: India relies on the Gulf for roughly 65% of its 🇮🇳 sulfur imports. 50% of the feedstock for companies like Coromandel, GSFC, and Paradeep Phosphates is under immediate threat, causing brutal margin pressure. Reduction in production is anticipated while imports drying out. The Bottom Line:📉 You cannot produce essential Diammonium Phosphate (DAP) or Monoammonium Phosphate (MAP) without sulfuric acid. As geopolitical choke points remain compromised, fertilizer companies reliant on non-integrated sulfur supply chains are bleeding.
