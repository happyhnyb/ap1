As one analyze markets looking for structural equilibrium, reviewing the global fertilizer trade data for the first week of April 2026, equilibrium is nowhere to be found. Instead, we are witnessing a profound "Nutrient Squeeze," where geopolitics, maritime chokepoints, and sovereign export limits have violently hijacked the pricing mechanism. For the sophisticated trader, the board is flashing red. The autopsy of four critical nutrients: 1. Urea: The Hormuz Premium ($750/MT) Urea has violently transitioned from an energy-cost story to a physical-availability crisis. With Middle East and Egyptian FOB prices surging to an astonishing $720-750/MT, the market is pricing in pure geopolitical fear. Congestion and severe disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz, compounded by Russian plant outages hence restrictions, have suffocated global export volumes. Even Southeast Asia is quoting FOB up to $710-730/MT, as producers like Vietnam fiercely prioritize domestic security over exports and China banning the exports. We are currently trading geopolitical risk, not just nitrogen. 2. DAP/MAP: The Sovereign Squeeze ($900/MT) Phosphates are trapped in a severe supply-side chokehold. With China deliberately restricting exports and running plants at a staggering low of 50–55% capacity, the global market is starving for tonnage. This sovereign intervention has forced Indian DAP CFR prices up to $830/MT from Australia, while Brazilian MAP has touched a brutal $900/MT CFR. While buyers in Southeast Asia are cautious at these levels, the absolute lack of alternative global origins is enforcing a formidable, unyielding price floor.

3. MOP: The Resilient Baseline ($400/MT) While Nitrogen and Phosphorus suffer from supply shocks, Potash is being driven by a resilient demand resurgence hitting a tight supply ceiling. We are seeing a firm $400/MT CFR across active buying markets in Brazil and Southeast Asia. 4. NPK: The Compounding Cost ($550/MT) Complex fertilizers are mathematically unforgiving; when the N and P components surge, NPK absorbs the blow. Driven entirely by these soaring input costs and constrained global supply due to Chinese ban on shipments of nitrogen-potassium blends and certain phosphate fertilizers prices has formed up. Meanwhile, relentless Indian demand has pushed their CFR prices to a steep $550/MT. The tragedy of the NPK complex right now is regional demand destruction due to farm-gate economics. The Bottom Line We have decisively exited a price-optimization market and entered a physical-security market.
