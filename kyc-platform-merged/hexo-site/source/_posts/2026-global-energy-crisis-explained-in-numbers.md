The recent escalations in West Asia are causing understandable global anxiety. When the world's energy heartland shakes, we all feel the tremors at the pump and in power grids. The candid reality of the energy crisis, strictly by the numbers. West Asia's Massive Supply Shock (March–April 2026)📉 The energy market is facing unprecedented turbulence due to infrastructure damage and export bottlenecks in the Gulf. The Oil Bleed🛢️ Shut-ins: 7.5 million barrels per day (bpd) offline ~7.5% of global supply wiped out in March. ➡️ Projected to hit 9.1 million bpd in April if disruptions persist. Export Plunge: Crude exports from major Gulf producers crashed by roughly 206 million barrels in March vs. February—a 44% regional decline. The Gas Gap🔥 Global Loss: ~20% of global gas supply is currently offline. Weekly Drain: Over 2 billion cubic metres (bcm) removed from the market. Monthly Plunge: Global exports dropped by about 5.8 million tonnes in March alone. Chokepoint Crisis: Strait of Hormuz disruptions + damaged infrastructure reduced LNG from Qatar & UAE by >300 million cubic metres/day. Infrastructure Under Fire💥 25+ major energy sites across 9 Gulf countries were targeted or severely damaged by, including critical Saudi facilities and Qatar’s massive Ras Laffan LNG plant.

Global Production & Deep Reserves🌍 Let's grasp the gravity of these disruption. Oil Production (Annual): ~36.5 Billion Barrels (~100 million bpd)🛢️ USA: ~20%🇺🇸 Saudi Arabia: ~12%🇸🇦 Russia: ~11%🇷🇺 Canada: ~6%🇨🇦 Iraq: ~5%🇮🇶 Reserves: Venezuela: ~17.5% (~300 billion barrels)🇻🇪 Saudi Arabia: ~17.2% (~297 billion barrels)🇸🇦 Iran: ~12% (~208 billion barrels)🇮🇷 Canada: ~10% (~168 billion barrels)🇨🇦 Iraq: ~8.4% (~145 billion barrels)🇮🇶 Natural Gas Production (Annual): ~4,100 Billion Cubic Metres (bcm)🔥 USA: ~24%🇺🇸 Russia: ~15%🇷🇺 Iran: ~6%🇮🇷 China: ~6%🇨🇳 Qatar: ~5%🇶🇦 Reserves: Russia: ~20% (~37 trillion cubic metres)🇷🇺 Iran: ~17% (~32 trillion cubic metres)🇮🇷 Qatar: ~13% (~24 trillion cubic metres)🇶🇦 United States: ~7% (~13 trillion cubic metres)🇺🇸 Turkmenistan: ~7% (~13 trillion cubic metres)🇹🇲 Middle East Dominance (Production Shares): The Middle East produces ~29% of global oil and ~18% of global natural gas. The Global Energy Pie🥧 Despite a heavy push for transition, we still live in a largely fossil-fueled world. Oil: ~31.5%🛢️ Coal: ~26.5%🪨 Natural Gas: ~23.5%🔥 Renewables: ~12.5% (Solar/Wind/Hydro)🌱 Nuclear: ~5%⚛️ Biomass/Other: ~1%🪵 Where Does All That Oil Go?🚛 Oil’s dominance is locked into transport and industry—sectors where quick, at-scale substitutes don't yet exist: Road Transport: ~45%🚗 Industry & Heating: ~25%🏭 Petrochemicals: ~15%🧪 Aviation: ~8%✈️ Shipping: ~7%🚢
