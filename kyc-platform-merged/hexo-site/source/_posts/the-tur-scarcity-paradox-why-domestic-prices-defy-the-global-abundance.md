The Indian Pigeon Pea (Tur) market is currently a textbook study in structural scarcity. While global origins are preparing for a massive surplus, the domestic landscape is defined by a widening gap between demand and a shrinking harvest. The Supply-Demand Reality Domestic demand is consistently upward of 4.0 MMT annually, driven by its status as a primary protein staple. However, production has fallen by 0.4 MMT to approximately 2.8 MMT. While the government holds a 0.6 MMT buffer, it serves only as a temporary tool for market intervention and a check on price manipulation. To bridge the remaining gap and maintain a healthy pipeline, India’s import requirement is an absolute 1.0 to 1.2 MMT this year—a figure that confirms a fundamentally bullish long-term outlook. Pricing: The "Desi Premium" vs. MSP Current Mandi prices sit at ₹72,000 - ₹75,000 per ton, trailing the ₹80,000 per ton MSP. However, as availability proves lower than expected, we anticipate domestic Tur will gravitate toward the ₹90,000–₹92,000 range. This "Desi Premium" reflects a supply constraint that harvest arrivals from April to June can only temporarily mask. With African supply not hitting our shores until August, the domestic floor remains structurally firm. The Global Glut & Institutionalized Imports Conversely, origin markets are facing a surplus. Myanmar has an estimated exportable surplus of 0.42 MMT, while the East and Horn of Africa boast a combined carry and anticipated production, based on farmers' intention, of 1.4 MMT. Yet, USD/INR constraints, volatile vessel

availability, high logistics/insurance costs, and lack of capital will be keeping the FOB prices range-bound at the origin. To counter global disruptions, India is institutionalizing its dependency through strategic 5-year MoU extensions beyond 2025–26: Myanmar: Securing 250,000 tons of Urad and 200,000 tons of Tur (including an additional 100,000 tons of dal starting in FY 2026-27). Mozambique & Malawi: Renewing commitments for 200,000 tons and 50,000 tons of Tur respectively. Policy & Currency Dynamics To facilitate this 1-1.2 MMT of committed imports flow, the government is maintaining a Zero- Duty regime to ensure availability. Furthermore, to bypass USD strength and liquidity traps, the INR-Kyat trade mechanism with Myanmar is being strengthened, allowing trade to flow despite currency volatility. The Bottom Line India is building a "Pulse Fortress." While the abundant global availability and dedicated MoUs cap the upside for imports, the domestic scarcity ensures a high-value premium for local produce.
