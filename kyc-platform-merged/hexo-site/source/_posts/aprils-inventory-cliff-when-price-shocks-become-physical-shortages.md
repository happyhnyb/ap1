If you thought the market disruptions in March following the US-Israel-Iran conflict were severe, there is some sobering news: You haven’t seen anything yet. While March was defined by a "Price Shock"—a frantic repricing of risk based on headlines—April is about to introduce us to the brutal laws of physics. We are moving from a crisis of Affordability to a crisis of Availability. In crisis one looks at the "buffers." In March, the global economy was still running on the "fat" of its strategic reserves assuming the military conflict to be short, as a sane world can't afford it. In April, we begin cutting into the bone. 1. The Nonlinear Risk of Empty Buffers Global reserves of oil, gas, fertilizers, and industrial raw materials act as the shock absorbers of civilization. But risk doesn't climb in a straight line. The "30-to-10" Rule: When your reserves drop from a 30 weeks supply to a 10 weeks supply, the "Risk Premium" doesn't just triple—it goes parabolic. The Urgency Premium: As buffers vanish, the "Cost of Urgency" takes over. It’s no longer about what a barrel of oil is worth; it’s about what you’re willing to pay to ensure your power grid doesn't collapse. 2. The Impossible Math of the "20% Gap" The conflict has effectively knocked out 20% of key energy production and access. In most industries, you can find a substitute or a workaround for a 5% shortfall. But 20%? That is a structural "Event Horizon." Energy is the "Master Resource." It is the primary feedstock for the Haber-Bosch process (give

fertilizers) and the literal fuel for every smelting and refining plant on earth. You cannot substitute 20% of global energy with "efficiency" or "renewables" in a 30-day or even 90-day window. When a quarter of the supply vanishes, the "Just-In-Time" logistics model doesn't just slow down—it shatters into a "Just-In-Case" hoarding frenzy. 3. The Bullwhip Effect on Food and Industry This is where the urgency becomes existential. The Fertilizer Lag: A fertilizer shortage in April isn't just a pricing problem today; it is a guaranteed yield crash for the harvest six months from now. Industrial Raw Materials: Without energy, basic processing halts. We are entering a "Cascading Failure" loop where the lack of energy prevents the production of the very material important to repair the economy. The Strategic Verdict: Paying for Time In March, we were paying for the commodity. In April, we are paying for Time. The buffers are diminishing, and as they deplete, the risk—and therefore the cost—accelerates. Demand remains inelastic because people still need to eat and stay warm, but the physical supply is simply not there. Even if war stops tomorrow, the destruction of physical infrastructure (refineries, ports, factories, and pipelines) means the "Supply Gap" will haunt us for years.
