While the harvest is hitting the mandis, the real story for the 2026 season isn't the arrival—it is the Mid-May to July Export Window. We are approaching a unique 75-day period where the domestic "Price Valley" will collide head-on with the global "Price Peak." A clear, high-stakes divergence is emerging between Indian internal supply mechanics and depreciating Indian rupees on one side and the fractured global landscape on another. The 107 MMT Reality vs. The Procurement Wall Our current estimate for the Indian wheat crop stands at 107 MMT. The Procurement Phase: The government is targeting 32–34 MMT for its central pool. Through April and early May, this state buying acts as a price floor, keeping domestic rates high and uncompetitive on the global stage. The "Free-Flow" Glut: The moment the government reaches its target and procurement centers begin to wind down (mid-May), the market will face a massive "Free-Flow" of the remaining ~73- 75 MMT. With the state no longer absorbing the surplus, the sheer volume in private hands will trigger a natural price correction. The Kharif Cash-Crunch: Why Farmers Will Sell Fast The secondary driver of this "Price Valley" is the anticipated high cost of Kharif cultivation. Input Inflation: With global energy markets and nitrogen supplies still reeling, the cost of diesel and fertilizer for the upcoming June/July sowing is expected to be record-setting. The Liquidation Trigger: Indian farmers do not have the luxury of "holding" their wheat for a Q3/4 price rally. They need immediate liquidity to fund the next crop cycle. This will lead to a

high-velocity liquidation of stocks in June, pushing domestic prices to their seasonal lows precisely when the global market is starving for calories. The Global Peak: Stressed Harvests and Nitrogen Gaps While India bottoms out in June, global wheat prices are anticipated to hit their highest point for the season. Northern Hemisphere Stress: The US Southern Plains are battling persistent drought, and Western Europe is dealing with excessive moisture and delayed planting. The Nitrogen Drag: The global "yield-drag" caused by the 2026 fertilizer shortage is finally being baked into futures. By June/July—the traditional "inter-crop" period—global inventories will be at their tightest, driving Chicago and MATIF prices toward a premium. The Strategic Play: Timing is the Only Metric The "Sweet Window" is a narrow door that slams shut in August when the massive Russian harvest hits the water. Accumulate in May/June: Capture the "Free-Flow" volume as government buying ends. Execute in June: Leverage the domestic price floor and the geographical dividend to Indian Ocean Rim. Exit by July: Clear stocks before Black Sea volumes suppress the global rally.
