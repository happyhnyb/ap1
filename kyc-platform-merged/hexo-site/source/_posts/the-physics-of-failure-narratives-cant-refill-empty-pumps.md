We are currently witnessing a masterclass in market manipulation, but the audience is starting to walk out. For the last four weeks, the global energy market has been held hostage by perception. We’ve seen a coordinated effort of "half-truths" and timed statements from global power centers designed to "change the course" of the market. They tell us the Strait of Hormuz closure is manageable. They tell us the price spikes are merely temporary blip. They tell us war has already been won just housekeeping is left. But in the energy world, narratives have a very short shelf life. Eventually, the brutal physics of availability and affordability stare you in the face. The Mirage of the Strategic Reserve The world is currently operating on its "last bullet." The recent IEA commitment to release 400 million barrels from Strategic Petroleum Reserves (SPR) is the largest coordinated draw in human history. But let’s be honest: this isn't a strategy; it’s a desperation move. When you deplete your strategic "savings account" to suppress a price narrative while production has fallen in percentage terms never seen in history, you aren't solving the problem— you are just delaying the inevitable. We are trading tomorrow’s energy security for today's headlines. From Inflation to "Energy Triage" The shift from a crisis of cost to a crisis of existence is already here. We are seeing: Declared Emergencies: Countries like the Philippines are already in a state of National Energy Emergency. Sri Lanka and Thailand are already reducing work days. South Korea is putting in

place retail price caps on fuel while India, Indonesia and Japan are rolling out large scale subsidies. Prioritization Over Productivity: Governments are now forced into "Energy Triage," prioritizing households over industrial zones. This isn't just a "slowdown"; it’s a structural productivity cut. Factories in Southeast Asia and Europe are moving to 4-day weeks simply to keep the lights on in hospitals. The Nitrogen Squeeze: The conflict in the Middle East has suspended 30% of the global ammonia- based nitrogen trade. We are no longer just talking about expensive fuel; we are talking about the potential for a global caloric deficit. The Symptom vs. The Cause We might have seen a momentary slowing of the price fall this week, but the "infestation" is spreading faster than we can comprehend. You cannot fix an energy emergency with a press release when the physical gateway—the Middle East—is on fire. If the root cause—the US-Iran-Israel conflict and the maritime blockade—is not resolved immediately, the symptoms will transition from high energy prices to empty grocery shelves. The "Narrative Era" of energy management has officially hit the wall of reality. The question today isn't "What is the price of oil?" but "Have you guaranteed physical supply for Q2/Q3?"
